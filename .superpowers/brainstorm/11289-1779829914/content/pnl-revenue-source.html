Where does "revenue" come from?

P&L needs a revenue number per project. The codebase has two possible sources.

Context

Costs already exist — they come from projection uploads (Vista ERP data) and are stored as ProjectionItems with cost-to-date, forecast, etc.

Revenue is the missing piece. We need to decide where it comes from for each project.

A

Invoices only

Revenue = sum of all invoice amounts for that project. Simple, concrete — it's what the customer was actually billed.

  • Already have invoice data linked to projects via projectId
  • Only counts billed revenue (draft invoices excluded or shown separately)
  • Straightforward: paid invoices = recognized revenue
B

Bid contract value

Revenue = the accepted bid's total. Represents the contract amount regardless of how much has been billed.

  • Gives a "total expected revenue" number upfront
  • Doesn't reflect actual billing progress
  • Could show both: contract value as the ceiling, invoiced as the floor
C

Both — layered view

Show contract value (from bid) as "Total Revenue" and invoiced amount as "Billed to Date". P&L shows margin against both.

  • Most complete picture: what you expected vs what you've billed vs what you've spent
  • More complex but matches how construction finance actually works
  • Contract value = estimate, invoiced = actual, delta = unbilled backlog
