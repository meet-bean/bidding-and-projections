Revenue is already in the data

The projection upload includes monthly revenue totals + original bid values

What the spreadsheet already provides

Monthly snapshots: revenue, cost, profit, GP% per month
Original bid: total revenue, cost, profit, GP% from the contract
Per-line-item detail: costs only (Labor, Material, Sub, Rental, etc.)

So the P&L view can use what's already imported — no new data source needed. The question becomes: what should the P&L view actually show?

What level of detail for the P&L?

Given that revenue is project-level and costs are per-line-item

A

Summary P&L only

Monthly revenue vs total cost, with profit and GP% per month. One row per month. Simple table + trend chart.

  • Matches the data granularity — revenue is already monthly totals
  • Clean, focused view: "are we making money on this project?"
  • Original bid comparison shows contract vs actual trajectory
B

Summary + cost breakdown

Top section: monthly revenue/cost/profit summary. Below: cost breakdown by type (Labor, Material, Sub, etc.) showing where the money goes.

  • Revenue stays as monthly totals (that's all we have)
  • Cost side drills down by cost type — leverages existing ProjectionItem detail
  • Answers both "are we profitable?" AND "where are we over budget?"
C

Full dashboard with charts

KPI cards (total revenue, total cost, GP%, variance from bid) + monthly trend chart + cost type breakdown + alerts for margin erosion.

  • Most visually rich — reuse projection engine's variance and alert logic
  • Heavier to build but makes the biggest demo impact
  • Could reuse existing trend modal SVG chart patterns
