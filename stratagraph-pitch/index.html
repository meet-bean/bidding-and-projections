The Bottom Line

Why MeetBean

We are technology builders. They are consultants. That one difference explains the product, the design, the support model, and why this relationship looks different after go-live.

1

Decades of building technology used by millions

The MeetBean team brings 10–20 years of experience building products at scale — consumer apps with millions of users, enterprise platforms serving complex operational environments. We know what it takes to ship software that works in the real world, not just in a demo.

That background shapes every decision: how the product is architected, how features are prioritized, how edge cases get handled, and what "done" actually means.

Consultants deliver projects. We have spent careers shipping products.

2

A better product

Design that people want to use

Fewer elements on screen. Workflows that match how people actually think. No training manuals required for a field crew to get started. The design comparison above illustrates the difference — and that difference compounds every day your team is in the platform.

SOPs via SharePoint is not a solution

Both Digineox and Zyntergy point to SharePoint for SOPs. The problem is well known: wrong versions get used, there is no sign-off workflow, nothing is auditable, and nobody knows if the document was actually read. MeetBean builds SOP management natively — write, version, assign, track, and audit, all inside the platform.

Integrations over custom builds

Where a connection to an existing system is the right answer, we use it — rather than proposing a custom build that adds cost, risk, and long-term maintenance burden. QuickBooks, Microsoft 365, SharePoint: connected, not rebuilt.

3

Support that never ends

The $12,500 annual fee includes support — every year, with no renegotiation and no surprise invoices when Year 1 closes.

The Transformation Partner is not a helpdesk. They are an ongoing resource embedded in your operations — expanding the platform into new workflows, replacing manual processes, and ensuring the team continues to get value long after go-live.

Most technology transformations stall because the vendor leaves once implementation is complete. The Transformation Partner model exists specifically to prevent that.

4

The future of software

The goal is to make Stratagraph independent — not to create dependency.

Phase 1 — Now

MeetBean builds rapidly, configures the platform, and trains deeply on Stratagraph workflows and operations.

Phase 2 — Near-term

The Stratagraph team gains confidence and begins creating enhancements, workflows, and automations themselves.

Phase 3 — Destination

Stratagraph builds full internal applications independently — no vendor required for day-to-day evolution.

5

A partnership — not a transaction

This is not a transaction. We see real opportunity here.

"How Stratagraph went from manual, fragmented processes to a fully digital, integrated operation — all consolidated under a single platform — enabling faster decisions, lower costs, and a scalable foundation for growth."

That story matters to us. Stratagraph becomes the flagship example of what this platform can do for a company in this space — and we intend to earn that story by delivering results that speak for themselves.
